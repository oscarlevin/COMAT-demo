Suppose \(f_1, f_2, \ldots, f_n\) are differentiable functions. Use mathematical induction to prove the generalized product rule:

\begin{equation*} (f_1 f_2 f_3 \cdots f_n)' = f_1' f_2 f_3 \cdots f_n + f_1 f_2' f_3 \cdots f_n + f_1 f_2 f_3' \cdots f_n + \cdots + f_1 f_2 f_3 \cdots f_n' \end{equation*}
